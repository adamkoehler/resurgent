Strategies to plan your business recovery

The Resurgent Collaborative was formed to assist companies in resurrecting their organization by recalibrating their business model and workforce and to plan for future health or event based business disruption.

As the apex of the Covid-19 approaches, companies need to plan for their business recovery.

Companies cannot afford to return to business as usual. The weaknesses of standard business systems and processes have been starkly revealed.

A resurgent American economy would do more to restore the confidence of the world in its own future than anything else. It begins with you, your employees, your stakeholders, and your business.

Who We Are

We have assembled an extraordinary team of experts to help you navigate through your recovery so that your business may emerge stronger than before. Our experts include Crisis Management and Media Relations professionals to help plan and address any crisis; Risk Management, Cyber Security for a remote workforce, IT Audit and compliance, Incident planning and response, business continuity and planning experts; Health risk planning and communication, checklist for reopening operations, financial, HR, Assurance and Legal services.

What We Do

Resurgent Collaborative can help you create solid plans during chaotic times. We have the communications, crisis planning, risk management and cyber security experts, international healthcare professionals, HR, assurance, legal and finance experts to help with every aspect of a business’ recovery. It is an unprecedented team of experts for an unprecedented time.

Your business recovery begins now.

Dan O’Keeffe

Dan is an experienced writer, editor, marketer, public relations professional, project manager, and website strategist with nearly 20 years experience in industries ranging from technology, professional services, pet products, consumer products, economic development, finance, legal, restaurant and hospitality, and Internet development.

Dan cut his teeth on public relations while researching and developing content for Ralston Purina’s many corporate communications magazines and newsletters in the early 1990s. Today, he specializes in helping organizations “tell their stories” and think strategically around how they position their brands, products and services with existing and new target audiences. Dan also advises small to large organizations and business and non-profit leaders on how to prepare for and respond to media inquiries during times of both boom and crisis.

Prior to entering the public relations and marketing fields, Dan edited two internationally circulated art-instruction magazines and worked as a newspaper and magazine reporter. Dan graduated from The University of Missouri-Columbia with a Bachelor of Journalism degree. He is a member of the Greater Cincinnati and Northern Kentucky Film Commission Board, serves on the Advisory Committee of the Professional Leadership Network, co-chairs the Accelerator program for the Cincinnati chapter of the Entrepreneurs Organization, co-founded the Institute for Social Media at Cincinnati State, served on the Marketing Committee for the Association for Corporate Growth, served on the advisory board of the Northern Kentucky Chamber of Commerce, and was named a Gentleman of Style and Substance by Cincinnati Magazine and the Cincinnati Parks Board.

He brings vast experience with all forms of business-to-business marketing, branding and creative communications, traditional and digital. He creates campaigns designed to achieve measurable growth, and has direct experience with Manufacturing, Home Building and Construction Products and Services, Information Technology, Life Sciences, Banking & Financial, Insurance, Professional Services and Non-Profit.

Dale earned the title of 2002 Man of the Year for the Leukemia and Lymphoma Society in 2002, serving on the Board of Trustees for six years. Dale earned a Bachelor of Science from the University of Cincinnati prestigious College of Design, Art, Architecture and Planning (DAAP). Dale and his wife live in suburban Cincinnati, spending as much time as possible with their three grandsons.

Rick Hackman

Rick Hackman is a native of Cincinnati and has spent the past 33 years working at Procter & Gamble leading the North America Regulatory and Technical External Relations Organization. Rick was responsible for leading key interactions with critical regulatory agencies, ensuring compliance with laws/regulations, developing trusting relationships with key external stakeholders to build and protect the business, and led crisis preparedness and business continuity planning (BCP) for P&G’s North America organization.

Rick was instrumental in helping resolve important product issues for P&G, including Pampers DryMax, Tide Pods, and several recalls. At O’Keeffe, Rick will help organizations evaluate their product and business risks, develop actions plans to mitigate identified risks, work with clients as they interact with government agencies, and develop business-building relationships with professional and technical thought leaders.

Rick obtained a bachelor’s degree in Biology and a master’s degree in Environmental Science from the University of Cincinnati, and an MBA in Finance from Xavier University. He is certified by the Institute for Professional Environmental Practice and the American Board of Industrial Hygiene. Rick and his wife enjoy spending time with their children and grandchildren. Rick is also a competitive triathlete, rower and avid weightlifter in his spare time.

Dr. Cherryl Christensen

Cherryl is former Corporate Medical Director for the Procter & Gamble Company and a board certified senior physician executive who excels at strategic thought and execution. She drove world-class health systems internationally for P&G and at the company's Cincinnati, Ohio headquarters. Cherryl designed P&G’s global health and wellness programs, oversaw the global EAP (work life effectiveness) program, travel medicine and executive health initiatives. Perhaps most notably, Cherryl managed global pandemic flu planning for P&G, co-creating the company’s first Pandemic Flu Preparedness Plan. She also prepared comprehensive disaster management plans with the Corporation’s security, industrial hygiene/safety and legal/regulatory teams.

Cherryl served on faculty at the University of Cincinnati Medical School for several years and continues to provide support in the field of occupational medicine through national lectures and articles in the role of corporate medical director for ACOEM.

Early in her career she provided medical care to the US Navy and Marines at Norfolk Naval Shipyard. Cherryl currently has her own consulting firm and has now joined forces with the collaborative to provide optimal COVID-19 business defense.

Cherryl also managed global Pandemic Flu planning while at P&G.
